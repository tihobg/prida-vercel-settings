Participation in the Fifth Youth Scientific Session "Biomedicine and Quality of Life" 2025

At the Fifth Youth Scientific Session "Biomedicine and Quality of Life", held on December 4-5, 2025 at the Institute of Biophysics and Biomedical Engineering at the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences, Sen. Assist. Prof. Ariana Langari presented a talk on the topic "Albumin Thermal Stabilization and Plasma Proteins Aggregation as Diagnostic Markers for Preeclampsia: Impact of Disease Severity" with co-authors Velichka Strijkova, Vesela Katrova, Ina Giosheva, Miroslava Ivanova, Svetla Todinova, Sashka Krumova, and Tania Pencheva. The talk demonstrated deviations in the temperature stability and aggregation of plasma proteins from patients with preeclampsia compared to healthy pregnant controls, which were more pronounced in the cases of severe preeclampsia. The following potential markers of the disease were identified: temperature stabilization of albumin, modification of the main plasma proteins as a result of oxidative stress and an increase in the size and number of plasma aggregates.
