„Severity-Dependent Modulation of Red Blood Cell Aging Patterns in Preeclampsia: Insights from Calorimetry and Atomic Force Microscopy“ (18.04.2026 г.)

On 18.04.2026 the International Journal of Molecular Sciences published the research article „Severity-Dependent Modulation of Red Blood Cell Aging Patterns in Preeclampsia: Insights from Calorimetry and Atomic Force Microscopy“ by Svetla Todinova, Velichka Strijkova, Ariana Langari, Ina Giosheva, Emil Gartchev, Vesela Katrova, Alexey Savov, Sashka Krumova, Tania Pencheva. The study was conducted in connection with the implementation of the project "Development of a Pathologic Pregnancy Database for Retrieval of New Knowledge for Diagnostic Markers Identification", funded by the European Union through the NextGenerationEU instrument under the Recovery and Resilience Mechanism in the implementation of investment C2I2 “Increasing the Innovation Capacity of the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences in the Field of Green and Digital Technologies”, part of the National Recovery and Resilience Plan. The presented results reveal important details regarding the structural and nanomechanical changes in red blood cells in patients with mild and severe preeclampsia. The application of biophysical methods atomic force microscopy and differential scanning calorimetry, as well as the statistical approach of linear mixed models demonstrated a significant destabilization of the hemoglobin - band 3 protein macrocomplex, which was most pronounced in the cases of severe preeclampsia. The presented approach allows the identification of early subclinical damage to red blood cells during pregnancy, which could not be detected by routine hematological tests.

Publication: Int. J. Mol. Sci. 2026, 27(8), 3633; https://doi.org/10.3390/ijms27083633
