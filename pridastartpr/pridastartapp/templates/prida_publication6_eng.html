Microfluidic system

In August 2025, a microfluidic system with biomedical applications was delivered to the Institute of Biophysics and Biomedical Engineering at the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences, in relation to the implementation of the project BG-RRP-2.011-0025 "Development of a Pathologic Pregnancy Database for Retrieval of New Knowledge for Diagnostic Markers Identification" (Pregnancy Related Integrated Database Analytics, PRIDA), funded by the European Union through the NextGenerationEU instrument under the Recovery and Resilience Mechanism in the implementation of investment C2I2 “Increasing the Innovation Capacity of the Bulgarian Academy of Sciences in the Field of Green and Digital Technologies”, part of the National Recovery and Resilience Plan. The microfluidic system is a high-quality imaging platform for conducting physiological analyses in flows up to 200 dyn/cm². The system consists of a BioFlux 1000HT electropneumatic pump for flow control, combined with a Zeiss Axiovert 5 inverted microscope. The selected configuration of the microfluidic system can find application in fields such as hematology, immunology, vascular biology, and microbiology, and provides opportunities for studying rheological properties of cells, biofilm formation, host-pathogen interactions, cell differentiation, chemotaxis, etc. According to the PRIDA project objectives, the microfluidic system will be applied to generate new data on the rheological properties of erythrocytes (deformability, aggregation) as indicators of microvascular disorders in pathological pregnancy, which will allow expanding the analysis of the relationships between clinical and biophysical characteristics in pathological pregnancy. Subsequently, these biophysical parameters will be entered into an integrated database with the aim of defining new prognostic markers/indicators.
