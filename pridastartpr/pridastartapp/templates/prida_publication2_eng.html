Participation in “XVI National Congress of Obstetrics and Gynecology with International Participation”

In the period April 02-05, 2026 the "XVI National Congress of Obstetrics and Gynecology with International Participation" was held (https://wasteels.bg/bg/congresno-subitie/khvi-nacionalen- kongres-po-akusherstvo-i-ginekologiya-s-mezhdunarodno-uchastie) at the Rila Hotel, Borovets, Bulgaria. At the congress, Ina Gyosheva, MD, presented a poster on the topic "Thermodynamic and Nanomechanical Alterations of Red Blood Cells in Preeclampsia: Cell Aging Effect" with co- authors Sashka Krumova, Velichka Strijkova, Ariana Langari, Alexey Savov, Vesela Katrova, Svetla Todinova, and Tania Pencheva. Combining differential scanning calorimetry and atomic force microscopy, the authors demonstrated that severe preeclampsia induces selective destabilization of the band 3 protein-hemoglobin macrocomplex in red blood cells, linking protein instability to impaired mechanical integrity of cells. From a translational perspective, the combined thermodynamic-nanomechanical approach to red blood characterization reveals new sensitive biophysical markers for identification of early red blood cells damage in patients with high-risk pregnancies that cannot be detected by routine hematological tests.
